Frequently Asked Questions

Quick explanations for entering your chit-fund details and understanding the results.

What information should I enter in the calculator?

Enter the number of members, monthly contribution, duration, fixed monthly expense, the month in which you receive the chit and the first instalment date. Then enter the actual bid discount for each month wherever it is available.

Where does the monthly dividend come from?

The winning member accepts less than the full pool by bidding a discount. After subtracting the entered fixed expense, the remaining discount becomes the dividend pool and is divided among the members according to the calculator’s assumptions.

How is the winner’s payout calculated?

The estimated payout is the total monthly pool minus the winning bid discount. The result therefore changes when the number of members, contribution amount or discount changes.

Do I stop paying after receiving the chit?

No. The calculator assumes that you continue making the required monthly contributions for the remaining term after receiving the payout, subject to the dividend adjustment for each month.

Why does the calculator use XIRR instead of CAGR?

CAGR is designed for one investment and one ending value over a regular period. A chit fund has multiple dated payments, changing dividends and one large payout, so XIRR is more suitable for estimating the annualised rate across those uneven cash flows.

What does the XIRR result mean?

XIRR is the annualised rate that makes the present value of all entered cash outflows and inflows balance. It should be interpreted together with whether the payout timing makes the arrangement behave more like borrowing or investing.

How should I read an early payout?

An early payout generally resembles borrowing: you receive a large amount near the beginning and continue paying instalments afterwards. In this case, the displayed annualised rate is better understood as an estimated financing cost rather than an investment return.

How should I read a late payout?

A late payout generally resembles investing: you make payments for much of the term before receiving the large inflow. In this case, the displayed annualised rate is closer to an estimated investment return.

What is the fixed monthly expense?

Use this field for the foreman’s commission or other fixed amount deducted before the balance of the monthly discount is distributed. Enter the amount specified by the chit fund’s records or agreement.

What does the payout-month comparison show?

It recalculates the cash-flow pattern for different possible payout months using the entered assumptions. This helps you compare how receiving the chit earlier or later changes the estimated cost or return.

Why might my actual return differ from this estimate?

Actual bid discounts, payment dates, missed payments, penalties, taxes, commissions, member defaults and the chit fund’s bye-laws can change the cash flows. Replace sample values with the actual records for a more meaningful estimate.

Can I rely on this calculator for a financial decision?

This calculator is an educational estimation tool. Verify the result against the registered chit agreement, auction statements, receipts and foreman records, and seek qualified advice before making a significant borrowing or investment decision.
